Why New Zealand

A real shortage.
A real opening.

New Zealand is currently short approximately 1,700 doctors — a gap that is projected to grow to 3,400 by 2032. Overseas-trained doctors already make up 43% of the registered workforce and 70–71% of all new registrations in recent years.

The demand is real. The opportunity is structural. And it is long-term.

"But knowing New Zealand needs doctors is only the starting point — knowing exactly how to get there is what makes the difference."

Every chapter has been reviewed against current MCNZ rules, Te Whatu Ora workforce data, and Immigration New Zealand policy. Nothing decorative. Every page exists because, without it, an IMG somewhere will miss a step that costs them a year.
