Part One

Why Australia.

The case, in numbers and in lived experience.

Australia is experiencing an unprecedented demand for medical professionals. Understanding what makes it attractive is the first step in your journey — and it is the question we will settle in this opening chapter before any document or exam is mentioned.

1. High demand for doctors

Australia's healthcare system is actively seeking qualified doctors, creating exceptional opportunities for IMGs ready to make the move.

16,900+
Vacant positions nationwide as of 2025 — across every specialty and region.
4
High-demand specialties: Emergency Medicine, General Practice, Psychiatry and Rural Medicine.
18%

28d

  • Structured training. Clear progression pathways with defined milestones.
  • Multi-department exposure. Opportunity to work across multiple departments as a junior doctor.
  • Strong mentorship. A culture of senior consultants guiding junior doctors.
  • Research opportunities. Conference funding support and academic research pathways.

2. Financial benefits that matter

Salary comparison, 2025 data.

  • Junior doctor salary. AUD $89,000 – $110,000 annually.
  • Earning multiplier. 2.8× more than Indian counterparts, against an INR 15 lakh average.
  • Overtime pay. Time-and-a-half for hours beyond the contracted 38–40 hours.
  • Weekend loading. Additional 50–100% pay for weekend shifts.
Real-world example

"In my first year as an HMO in Queensland, I earned AUD $95,000 base salary plus AUD $18,000 in overtime. After taxes, I saved AUD $35,000 — more than my entire annual salary back home."

— Dr. Anika, Mumbai

3. The work-life balance revolution

Unlike many healthcare systems worldwide, Australia prioritises sustainable medical practice with strong protections for doctors.

  • 38–40 hour week. The contracted workweek — not the 60–80 hours common elsewhere.
  • 4 weeks annual leave. Mandatory to take. Rest is built into the system.
  • Professional development leave. 2 weeks paid leave annually for CPD and upskilling.
  • Sick leave. Generous allowances without penalty — your health comes first.
  • 76% of IMGs report better quality of life compared to their home country.
  • World-class Medicare. Access to Australia's public healthcare system from day one.

4. Straightforward immigration pathways

Australia offers one of the clearest paths to permanent residency for medical professionals — a structured progression from work visa to citizenship.

  1. 01
    Skills in Demand Visa (Subclass 482)

    Initial work visa with employer sponsorship — your entry point.

  2. 02
    Permanent Residency (Subclass 190 / 189)

    Available after 2–3 years of work experience in Australia.

  3. 03
    Citizenship

    After 4 years total — including at least 1 year as a Permanent Resident.

  • Priority processing. Healthcare workers average just 28 days for visa processing.
  • Age limit removed. No age ceiling for doctors under the Skills in Demand visa.
  • Family inclusion. Spouse can work; children can study in Australian schools.
  • Extended family options. A path to bringing parents on extended visitor visas.
Part Two

Understanding the three AMC pathways.

Australia recognises three distinct routes for IMGs. The right one depends on where you trained, what you have already passed, and how senior you are.

Important

Your pathway choice is effectively permanent once you begin the process. While you can switch pathways in exceptional circumstances, it involves additional costs and delays. Therefore, understanding each pathway thoroughly before committing is crucial for your success.

Pathway 1 — Standard Pathway Most common · 78% of IMGs

Who it is for:

  • MBBS / MD graduates who have completed an internship in their home country.
  • Graduates from medical schools listed in the International Medical Education Directory (IMED).
  • Those seeking general registration to practice as junior doctors.

The two-step assessment:

  1. 01
    AMC CAT MCQ Examination

    150 multiple-choice questions over 3.5 hours. Computer-adaptive format covering Medicine, Surgery, O&G, Pediatrics, Psychiatry, and Public Health. Current pass rate: 50%. Cost: AUD $2,920.

  2. 02
    AMC Clinical Examination — or Workplace-Based Assessment

    Clinical Exam: 16 assessed stations. Pass requirement: 9 / 14 stations (updated March 2024). Pass rate: 35%. Cost: AUD $3,991 in-person, AUD $4,391 online.

    WBA alternative: 6–12 months supervised practice. 98% success rate. Cost: AUD $12,000. Waiting time: 6–18 months depending on location.

Pathway 2 — Competent Authority Pathway Fast-track · 15% of IMGs

Who it is for:

  • Medical graduates who have completed recognised examinations in the UK, Ireland, USA, Canada, or New Zealand.
  • Those with 1–2 years of supervised work experience in these countries.

Qualifying examinations by country:

  • UK / Ireland. PLAB 1 & 2 plus 1 year supervised practice.
  • USA. USMLE Steps 1, 2 & 3 plus 2 years supervised practice.
  • Canada. MCCQE plus 1 year supervised practice.
  • New Zealand. NZREX plus 1 year supervised practice.
  • Bypass AMC examinations entirely. Direct pathway to general registration — no MCQ or Clinical Exam required.
  • Faster processing. 12–18 months versus 18–24 for the Standard Pathway.
  • Lower costs. AUD $8,000 – $10,000 versus $14,000 – $15,000 for Standard.
2025 fast-track update

New expedited processing for PLAB / USMLE holders with 2+ years experience — registration now possible in just 6 months.

Pathway 3 — Specialist Pathway (SIMG) Advanced · 7% of IMGs

Who it is for:

  • Specialists with 4+ years post-graduation experience.
  • Those seeking specialist recognition or area-of-need positions.
  • Consultants wanting to practice at specialist level in Australia.

The assessment process:

  1. 01
    College Assessment

    Evaluation by the relevant Australian specialist college — RACP, ACEM, RANZCOG and so on.

  2. 02
    Documentation Review

    Detailed logbook of procedures, supervision records and teaching experience.

  3. 03
    Potential Supervised Practice

    6–24 months depending on the college's assessment outcome.

2025 expedited options — priority specialties:

  • Anaesthesia and Pain Medicine. 6-month assessment timeline.
  • Psychiatry. Fast-track for rural positions.
  • Emergency Medicine. Priority for regional hospitals.
  • Rural General Practice. Accelerated pathway for rural areas.

Part Four

Common myths, debunked.

Myth 1
"Australia doesn't recognise Indian medical degrees."

Reality. Indian degrees from MCI / NMC-recognised institutions are fully accepted. Over 12,000 Indian doctors currently practice in Australia. The key is proper documentation through EPIC verification.

Myth 2
"You need Australian clinical experience to get hired."

Reality. Many hospitals hire IMGs directly after AMC MCQ + English test for limited registration positions. Australian clinical experience helps but is not mandatory for first jobs.

Myth 3
"Regional jobs mean isolation and poor facilities."

Reality. Regional centres like Geelong (1 hour from Melbourne) or Newcastle (2 hours from Sydney) offer excellent facilities, shorter commutes and stronger community support.

Myth 4
"AMC exams are impossible to pass."

Reality. With proper preparation, success is achievable. AMC MCQ has a 50% pass rate, AMC Clinical 35%, and WBA has a 98% success rate for those who can access it.

Myth 5
"Australia is too expensive for junior doctors."

Reality. While living costs are high, junior doctor salaries are proportionally higher. Most IMGs save AUD $25,000 – $35,000 annually even after all expenses.

Part Five

Your first 90 days.

A clear, structured plan for your first three months — to build the strongest possible foundation for your Australian medical journey.

Weeks 1–2 · Foundation setup

  • Create EPIC account at ecfmg.org/epic.
  • Gather required documents: academic transcript, internship completion certificate with exact dates, medical degree.
  • Set up finances — ensure access to a credit card for international payments.
  • Tip. Use original degree certificate — provisional certificates are rejected in 23% of cases.

Weeks 3–4 · Registration and planning

  • Submit EPIC verification — processing time is 2–3 business days.
  • Create AMC portfolio using your EPIC ID.
  • Choose your English test — PTE is recommended for being cheaper and having higher pass rates.
  • Join IMG communities: the "Indian Doctors in Australia" Facebook group has over 45,000 members.

Months 2–3 · Preparation and strategy

  • Book the AMC MCQ exam — aim for 6–8 months of preparation.
  • Start English test preparation after AMC MCQ for optimal timing.
  • Research target locations — identify preferred states and hospitals.

Preparation resources

  • Free. AMC Handbook and the official AMC website practice questions.
  • Paid. AMCQBank ($899 / 6 months), PassMedicine Australia ($650 / year).
